# Alpha Value: NLP View

1. **S3 is partially supported, but only in a limited sense.** In NLP, decomposition often helps when the intermediate representation is task-aligned: CoT can improve hard reasoning, and finance papers like **LLMFactor**, **ECC Analyzer**, **Mental Models**, and **Structured Event Representation** show that staged extraction can improve downstream prediction or interpretability. But this is **not** the same as proving that CoT reduces memorized-outcome leakage. The strongest defensible claim is: decomposition can prime the model to attend to article-local variables before predicting, which may reduce contamination, but that mechanism still needs direct testing.

2. **LLMFactor is the same family as S3, not the same claim.** Wang et al. use **Sequential Knowledge-Guided Prompting**: first generate background knowledge, then extract stock-movement factors from news, then combine those factors with price history for prediction. They report better results than keyphrase, sentiment, and prior SOTA baselines across four U.S./Chinese datasets, and their ablations say the **factor layer contributes most of the MCC gain**. So it supports “decomposition before prediction can help.” It does **not** directly test “decomposition reduces leakage.” Also, their setup is less strictly text-grounded than yours because it explicitly generates background knowledge, whereas your authority/novelty prompts ban outside knowledge and later outcomes.

3. **There is a principled NLP case for predictive value beyond “richer is better.”** Sentiment compresses many distinct variables into one polarity score. Text-grounded features can isolate latent dimensions that sentiment conflates: provenance/attribution (**authority**), disclosure framing and freshness (**novelty**), event structure, relations, and influence. That matters because markets react differently to fresh vs stale information (Tetlock-style novelty) and because broader story-level text beats flat sentiment in classical text-asset-pricing work (Heston-Sinha; Ke-Kelly-Xiu). In NLP terms, decomposition reduces aliasing, gives the downstream model cleaner low-dimensional slots, and allows calibrated abstention via `unclear` instead of forced directional guesses.

4. **Low intrusion does not automatically imply usefulness.** If direct prediction shows high post-publication outcome intrusion but authority shows low intrusion, authority is a **cleaner measurement** of article-grounded evidence. That improves construct validity and trustworthiness as an input channel. But it could still be weak or irrelevant for returns. So separate two axes: **reliability** (faithful, low contamination) and **utility** (incremental association with returns, complementarity to sentiment, stability under shift). Low intrusion is necessary for a clean signal claim, not sufficient for an alpha claim.

5. **For EMNLP, frame this as a representation-utility claim, not a backtested-alpha claim.** Say: authority and novelty are **economically motivated, text-grounded, lower-leakage candidate features**. Without a full backtest, support “usefulness” with intrinsic evidence: low outcome intrusion under false-outcome / counterfactual tests, human agreement on slots, orthogonality or complementarity to sentiment, and stability under matched-schema/paraphrase controls. The bounded paper claim is: these factors are **better-behaved signal inputs** than direct prediction, and therefore plausible building blocks for downstream forecasting.
