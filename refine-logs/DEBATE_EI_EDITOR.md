This critique is serious but not fatal. As written, EI is not cleanly identified: `cf_invariance - para_invariance` mixes leakage sensitivity with perturbation magnitude, because the counterfactual edits are usually span-local while the paraphrases are broader rewrites. Blocks 2-3 help, but they do not fully discharge this. The Stage-2 QC overlap in normalized Levenshtein bands and the matched-format/sham controls address format burden, output schema, and some style drift; they do not directly equalize locality or edit distance between the minuend and subtrahend in EI.

At a top venue, I would not be satisfied with authors merely acknowledging the confound and asserting it is bounded. This is too close to a construct-validity problem for the paper's primary metric. A hostile reviewer could plausibly say the paper has shown robustness to small semantic edits minus robustness to large paraphrases, not memorization.

The paper survives if the authors add one concrete control showing the ordering persists after perturbation matching. The minimum fix is to condition EI on perturbation magnitude: either (i) add a local-paraphrase control matched to the counterfactuals on changed-span count / edit-distance bins, or (ii) reweight / residualize EI using normalized Levenshtein, changed-span count, sentence delta, and length ratio, then show the task ordering holds within matched bins or after adjustment. I would also want a small audit confirming the paraphrase condition preserves task labels.

If that robustness check preserves `direct > sentiment > decomposition`, the metric becomes defensible. Without it, the main mechanism claim remains vulnerable.
