The paper is close to over-scoped. EMNLP papers can sustain about three experiment families if they all sharpen one claim; beyond that, reviewers stop seeing triangulation and start seeing a grab-bag. Right now you have benchmark construction, behavioral diagnosis, causal intervention, temporal analysis, and prompt-level mitigation. That is one experiment family too many unless some are clearly demoted to appendix.

If I had to keep three in the main paper, I would keep: (1) CLS-Leak benchmark construction, because it is the reusable artifact and makes all comparisons apples-to-apples; (2) CF label sensitivity, because it is the cleanest core leakage metric; and (3) false-outcome continued pretraining, because it is the strongest causal anchor showing that the task can gate access to implanted outcome memory. Evidence intrusion can stay as a light portability check or appendix. Temporal discontinuity is the first thing I would cut if time tightens.

The priming test belongs in this paper only in a narrow role: as a small mechanism or mitigation bridge showing that leakage is not just detectable but prompt-sensitive. It should not become a full second storyline with many prompt variants, models, and ablations. Once it turns into “here is a mitigation recipe,” you are writing a different paper with a different review burden. My recommendation is one compact section or appendix figure now, then a follow-up if the effect is large.

There is a plausible paper centered on priming: “text-grounded decomposition reduces look-ahead leakage.” But that paper would need a different evidentiary backbone. You would need stronger prompt baselines, multiple models, utility-preservation checks, and a sharper faithfulness framing. Otherwise reviewers may dismiss it as a prompt hack. In other words: yes, but only by demoting CPT from headline to supporting mechanism and rewriting the contribution around context faithfulness.

For 10 weeks, the realistic version is: freeze the benchmark slice immediately; finish CF sensitivity and the Qwen false-outcome CPT as the main story; run one tightly scoped priming study on the same slice; move evidence intrusion and temporal discontinuity to appendix if they are already easy, and cut them otherwise. That scope is viable. A version that tries to make all six layers feel equally central is not.
