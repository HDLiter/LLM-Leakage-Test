1. **Type A/B:** This makes the paper stronger, provided you keep Type A/B as a light conceptual distinction rather than a new formal taxonomy. It fixes a reviewer problem in the current proposal: not all memory is bad. Type A is legitimate background competence; Type B is evaluation-invalidating look-ahead recall. That also rehabilitates counterfactual label sensitivity: reversing article semantics should move answers supported by article-grounded reasoning plus general priors, but should not systematically rescue answers driven by memorized future outcomes. The risk is only complexity creep if you start claiming you can separately measure A and B directly.

2. **Different paper?** Yes, this is now a meaningfully different paper, and the proposal should be rewritten. The old center of gravity was measurement: compare detectors and task types. The new center is mechanism: task design opens or closes the path from parametric memory to the answer. The dataset, experiments, and methods can stay mostly the same, but the abstract, introduction, RQ1, and theory section should foreground pathway control rather than "how much contamination exists."

3. **Positioning:** Relative to Wu et al. *Reasoning or Reciting?*, your paper is more specific and, if executed cleanly, more mechanistic. Wu et al. show that task reformulation can distinguish reasoning from reciting default conventions. You are showing which kind of stored knowledge is being elicited: useful domain priors versus temporally illicit outcome memory. Relative to Li et al. on context-faithfulness, this is the closest intellectual neighbor. Their result is that evidence style and memory strength determine whether models follow context or memory. Your addition is temporal contamination: outcome-proximal prompts let memory answer directly, whereas authority-style prompts make that memory less decision-relevant.

4. **One-sentence contribution:** We show that look-ahead leakage is not just a property of a model or dataset, but of the task pathway: on the same financial news, outcome-proximal prompts expose memorized future outcomes, while evidence-grounded prompts suppress that channel and preserve usable background knowledge.

5. **Venue calculus:** Yes, this improves the venue calculus. As a pure measurement/benchmark paper, Findings was the natural home. As a mechanism paper with a clean same-article design and a general claim about task pathways, main EMNLP becomes plausible. I would still treat Findings as the median outcome unless the paper is simplified aggressively and the evidence is very clean across models and controls.

6. **Revised title:** *Task Design Gates Look-Ahead Memory: Outcome-Proximal Prompts Amplify Leakage in Financial News LLMs*
