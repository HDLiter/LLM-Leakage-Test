## Revisions

1. **On CFLS true negative:** Agrees. A floor at/below zero is not itself evidence CFLS is broken. Problem is narrower: with no positive controls, pilot cannot distinguish **true absence of signal** from **insensitive measurement rule**. This is a calibration failure, not necessarily a design failure. The Challenger's positive-control diagnostic is exactly right.

2. **On CPT and persuasion:** Strengthens position. CPT, even with placebo controls, does not identify memorization in isolation. Excess effect over placebo is still a mixture of memorization, semantic anchoring, and in-context persuasion. **No clean exclusion restriction.** Becomes useful only when **interacted with an exposure proxy** — if flip rates are systematically higher for likely-exposed items than matched unlikely-exposed items, that differential is more informative than raw flip rate.

3. **On temporal split:** Agrees this is the strongest identification proposal. Pre/post cutoff creates exogenous variation in exposure probability — far closer to valid quasi-experiment than semantic reversal. Caveats:
   - Pre/post cases differ in recency, prominence, news diffusion → need matched or regression-adjusted comparison
   - If cutoff adherence is imperfect → fuzzy design, not sharp
   - Still much more defensible than CFLS under bidirectional generalization

4. **On three cheap diagnostics:** Agrees with sequencing. These answer the key econometric question: is there any detectable exposure-linked variation to measure?

5. **On Simpson's paradox (medium > high CFLS):** Elaborated confounders:
   - **Event rarity:** high-likelihood items may be common two-sided events → stronger semantic-following
   - **Category mix:** policy/macro have more canonical bullish/bearish opposites
   - **Counterfactual plausibility:** some reversals are linguistically more natural
   - **Base uncertainty:** uncertain model → larger flips from small prompt changes
   - **Measurement error in "memorization likelihood":** label partly built from prominence, proxies both exposure AND bidirectional coverage

6. **Revised claim:** Pilot does not show CFLS is invalid; it shows CFLS is **unvalidated and confounded** under current design. Positive controls + exposure variation are the necessary next step.
